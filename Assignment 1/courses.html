Courses

Computer Essentials

CST8101

Level 1

Students will get a fundamental overview of the Windows Operating System and learn how to install, configure, and troubleshoot difficulties. They will learn about computer organization, including fundamental numerical systems, as well as the hardware and software components required to run programs. On a simple PC, students will also generate backups, install virus protection, and manage data.

Introduction to Computer Programming

CST8116

Level 1

Students learn the basics of computer programming, with a focus on problem analysis and design. To develop problem-solving ideas, the Java programming language is employed. Practical lab tests and laboratory exercises for real-life applications strengthen theory.

Introduction to Database

CST8215

Level 1

Students study the principles of relational databases and how to design, manage, and query databases using Structured Query Language (SQL). Students learn about the operations and components of a database management system (DBMS). Learn about several types of data storage and how they interact with older systems.

Achieving Success in Changing Environments

CST8300

Level 1

Life in the twenty-first century offers several opportunities and ethical concerns, as well as possible perils and ethical issues. Students examine their own abilities and capabilities, as well as critical thinking and decision-making skills, to assist in the resolution of some of society's most pressing challenges.

Communications I

ENL1813T

Level 1

Students improve and build communication skills that help them succeed in both the classroom and the business. Students focus on achieving the needs of successful communication using a practical, vocation-oriented approach. Students practice writing, speaking, reading, and listening through a combination of lectures, activities, and individual study.

Technical Mathematics for Computer Science

MAT8001C

Level 1

Calculus requires a thorough understanding of algebraic and transcendental functions. Students learn how to manipulate algebraic expressions, solve algebraic equations and linear systems, and graph algebraic and transcendental functions. To answer difficulties with computers, students look at computer number systems as well as Boolean algebra and logic. Students also learn how to use vector components to add and subtract vectors.

Database Systems

CST2355

Level 2

Students gain hands-on experience with industry-standard database management systems such as Oracle and MySQL. Advanced SQL, case structures, rollup and cube operations, metadata manipulation, security and transaction management, and data warehousing are among the topics addressed.

Operating System Fundamentals (Gnu/Linux)

CST8102

Level 2

In theory and via practical tasks, students investigate the specifics of operating system structures, process management, storage management, installation, configuration, and administration. The lab work is meant to help students put theory into practice by teaching them how to use the powerful GNU/Linux command-line tools and utilities.

Object Oriented Programming (Java)

CST8284

Level 2

Working in the field of information technology necessitates a thorough knowledge of Object-Oriented Programming (OOP). Encapsulation, inheritance, abstraction, and polymorphism are all discussed. Students learn the fundamentals of data structures and algorithms, as well as how to develop a graphical user interface.

Web Programming

CST8285

Level 2

The World Wide Web has become ingrained in our daily lives. Students learn the fundamentals of web development, web design, and website implementation. Practical tasks that encourage students to build and manage their own websites supplement the lectures.

Technical Communication for Engineering Technologies

ENL2019

Level 2

In a technically focused multidisciplinary workplace, students are taught how to communicate successfully. Students are exposed to activities and assignments that promote critical thinking, research, writing, visual communication, and presenting abilities linked to technical issues in an autonomous and collaborative manner.

Cooperative Education Readiness

GEP1001

Level 2

Students are led through a number of exercises that will help them prepare for their co-op job hunt. Students participate in workshop-style sessions to explore their strengths and transferrable abilities. Students learn how to use HireAC, a website where companies advertise cooperative education employment openings.

Trends in Today's Workplace

MGT7330

Level 2

In today's work culture, every employee must be aware about contemporary workplace trends and challenges. Students analyze the reality of social networking, workplace diversity, and job mobility as they investigate new concerns confronting employees in today's technology-driven workplace.
